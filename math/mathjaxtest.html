Prime Numbers and Encryption

Definition 1. An integer \( p > 1 \) is called a prime if its only divisors are 1 and \( p \) itself.

The number 75 can be written as \( 3 \times 5^2 \). The numbers 3 and 5 here are called the prime factors of 75.

The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic says that any integer \( n > 1 \) has a unique representation as a product of primes.

Euclid’s Theorem: There are infinitely many primes.

Primality Check with Trial Division

To test if a number \( n \) is prime, check divisibility up to \( \sqrt{n} \). This is effective for small numbers, but not practical for large ones.

Modular Arithmetic

We say \( a \equiv b \ (\text{mod} \ m) \) if \( m \mid (a - b) \).

Example: \( 18 \equiv 2 \ (\text{mod} \ 4) \) since \( 18 = 2 + 4 \times 4 \).

If \( a \equiv b \ (\text{mod} \ m) \), then \( a^n \equiv b^n \ (\text{mod} \ m) \).

Fermat’s Primality Test

Fermat's Little Theorem: If \( p \) is prime and \( a \) is any integer, then \( a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \ (\text{mod} \ p) \).

This is used to test whether \( n \) is probably prime by checking if \( a^{n-1} \equiv 1 \ (\text{mod} \ n) \).

Prime Numbers in RSA Encryption

Choose primes \( p \) and \( q \). Let \( n = p \times q \), and \( \lambda = (p-1)(q-1) \).

Choose \( e \) such that \( \gcd(e, \lambda) = 1 \), and compute \( d \equiv e^{-1} \ (\text{mod} \ \lambda) \).

Encryption: \( C \equiv M^e \ (\text{mod} \ n) \)
Decryption: \( M \equiv C^d \ (\text{mod} \ n) \)
